Smooth moves, in code.

A timeline-driven animation engine for the canvas. Motion that's buttery in preview, light on the server, and built on ideas that humans and models already get.

Essence

Effortless machinery,
so the result just glides.

The hard parts (layout, timing, server-side rendering) stay out of sight. What's left is motion that feels good to watch and good to write. The wink is in the name. The substance is in the engine.

01 / Color

Three brights, two calm.

Coral, mint and a pop of sunshine do the playing. Grape ink and warm cream keep things grounded so the candy never gets sickly. Bright where it counts, calm everywhere else.

Coral
#FF5640

Primary. Gradient start, the loud, friendly hello. Buttons and big moments.

Sunshine

Default canvas. Warm, not clinical. The calm behind the motion.

The Smoove Gradient
The hero. Coral melting into mint, like motion frozen mid-glide. Use it on the mark, key accents, and one focal surface per screen. Never as a full-page wash.

02 / Mark, wordmark & type

The double-o is the gift.

Lowercase, rounded, friendly geometric sans. The rounded terminals echo eased motion, with no hard stops. The two o's ghost into an afterimage: movement, frozen.

The mark: four timeline tracks tapering to a play triangle, with a sunshine keyframe arriving just past the last bar. The gradient version leads; the one-color light and dark cuts keep the yellow dot at all times.

Do
  • Keep it lowercase, always.
  • Let the afterimage trail to the left, as if it glided in.
  • Give it room: generous clear space on all sides.
Don't
  • Capitalize, condense, or outline the letters.
  • Apply the gradient to body-size type.
  • Stack ghosts so dense the word stops reading.

03 / How products should look

If a shape could be a keyframe
in a smooth animation, it belongs.

Five rules that turn the brand into an interface: light, sharp surfaces, eased curves, generous space, motion as the through-line, and code treated as the hero instead of being hidden away.

Motion as identity

Every transition is a chance to show the afterimage idea: trails, eased entrances, things that glide in rather than appear.

Eased curves & air

Nothing linear, nothing cramped. Rounded corners, soft easing, generous whitespace. Comfortable, never rigid.

Aao

Rounded geometric type

Comfortaa for display & the wordmark, Hanken Grotesk for text, JetBrains Mono for code. Friendly headers, sharp body.

Light & sharp surfaces

Paper-warm backgrounds, crisp 1px borders, restrained shadows. The gradient is the only loud thing on screen.

Code as hero

Show, don't boast. A tight sample beats an adjective. Mono blocks get the gradient accent and room to breathe, and the docs read clean enough that a model can follow them literally.

05 / Voice

Plain, a little playful.

Do

Short sentences, active verbs. Lean on the motion metaphor: glide, flow, frame, timeline. Show with a sample. Write so a model can follow it literally.

Don't

Name or compare to other libraries. Overclaim with numbers you haven't measured. Get cute at the cost of clarity. The joke is the name, not the docs.
